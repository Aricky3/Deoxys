Problem Description


What is Mirror box therapy?

Mirror box therapy is a treatment technique typically used for patients experiencing phantom limb pain, motor recovery after stroke, or other rehabilitation needs. The method involves a box with a mirror placed in the center. The patient places the intact limb within one side of the box and then moves it; the reflection creates the illusion of a complete, healthy limb, which can “trick” the brain to help alleviate pain and help with recovery.



Current Problems

Despite its effectiveness, traditional mirror box therapy has limitations. The main issue is the need for regular in-person sessions with a healthcare provider, which can be challenging for patients with busy schedules, mobility issues, or geographical limitations. Further, the nature of the mirror box limits the range of motion and comfort of the therapy at the time of therapy.



Our Product/Solution: Virtual Reality Mirror Therapy

Our solution tackles these problems head-on by introducing Virtual Reality (VR) into mirror box therapy. Our product is a virtual reality mirror therapy application that can be used at home or any convenient location, using a VR headset.



Here's how our product helps to address the current problems:

  • Accessibility: Our VR solution allows patients to undergo therapy sessions no matter where they are. It reduces the need for regular, in-person visits to healthcare providers, making it especially useful for those with irregular work schedules or limited mobility.
  • Engagement: Traditional mirror therapy relies heavily on the patient's ability to "imagine" the movement of the absent or affected limb. Our VR solution can visually replicate the limb in various environments and contexts, increasing immersiveness, patient engagement and potentially boosting how effective the therapy can be.
  • Customizability: Our product can be tailored to each patient's specific needs, allowing for personalized therapy sessions. The VR program can be adjusted to the user’s or the clinician’s preferences to boost how effective the therapy can be.

Feedback Session Results

The expert we discussed with was Brennan Marshall, a 3rd year medical student at UCSD Health and works at the simulation center. He has a background in the intersection between computer science and medicine, which gives him an avid understanding in this field. He has developed a mirror therapy app on iOS but this inherently limits the user's experience since they are bound to the 2D device, making it less user-friendly. We followed a semi-structured interview process for our feedback session. Since our prototype was a 3D application, we recorded a video showing what it would look like. We shared this video with our expert at the very beginning of our feedback session, and asked questions about it afterwards. The expert brought up many points that were interesting and relevant to our prototype, so we ended up diverging rather far from our initial interview questions, but circled back towards them at the end of the feedback session. The main feedback points we received are listed below:

  • The mirrored limb has to be very realistic.
  • He suggested a scanning method, where real time image processing is used to mirror limbs
  • He suggested that hand tracking can be used to inform the image processing
  • Something as simple as the lack of a tattoo (or an extra tattoo) can render the therapy less effective, so realism should be a priority.
  • A normal VR avatar would not be sufficient.
  • The convenience of the setup will be the main driver for user use, and a complex PC-connected sort of setup might end up an interesting project but with nobody using it
  • AR is not feasible for this project, because users will be able to see their lack of a limb clearly, breaking the therapy.
  • We need an easier and more intuitive interface to pick out missing limb
  • Another large benefit of this would be data collection for researchers and clinicians
  • We could use other devices to remind the user to do the therapy

From the feedback, the main takeaways that we applied were:

  • A diagram for users to pick which limb is missing
  • Scanning the users limb that they wish to do mirror therapy in order to effectively and accurately reproduce limb
  • Guided sessions within the app for ease of use
  • Notifications on a smartphone or connected smart watch to remind the user to check in
  • Interface for researchers and clinicians to access the session data of the users
  • Making sure the environment is as realistic as possible

Features

Interactive Virtual Reality Mirror Therapy

Unveil an immersive rehabilitation experience with our innovative VR software, designed to mirror real-time therapy sessions. With the power of VR, users can control a digital version of their affected limb, stimulating neural pathways to enhance recovery. Its immersiveness has the potential to be more engaging and effective than traditional mirror box therapy.

Personalized Therapeutic Exercises

Our VR software is not one-size-fits-all. It allows users to tailor their therapeutic exercises, considering their comfort, recovery stage, and specific needs. The adaptive VR environment modifies the virtual tasks to match individual requirements, providing a personalized therapy regimen for each user, regardless of their condition or recovery stage.

Real-time Progress Tracking and Analytics

Equipped with real-time progress tracking, our software provides users with insightful feedback on their recovery journey. Using smart algorithms, it records and analyses each therapy session, delivering comprehensive data on user improvement. This allows the user to progress at home and at their own pace, while clinicians are able to monitor and examine their progress log.

Frequently Asked Questions

After conducting our expert feedback session, we realized how important it was to not break the experience for the user. Even a missing tattoo, or a tattoo present where none had been before, could break the immersion and ruin the effectiveness of the therapy. Current AR technology would let the user see their affected limbs, and break the immersion.
The limb will be a 3D model, scanned from the limb to be mirrored, and will also be customizable with a number of pre-made options such as tattoo removal/addition, to boost immersion.
The VR Mirror Therapy software comes with a built-in progress tracking feature. It records and analyses your performance during each session using advanced algorithms, providing insightful data about your improvement over time. This feature helps you understand your recovery trajectory better, allowing for necessary adjustments to your therapy plan
